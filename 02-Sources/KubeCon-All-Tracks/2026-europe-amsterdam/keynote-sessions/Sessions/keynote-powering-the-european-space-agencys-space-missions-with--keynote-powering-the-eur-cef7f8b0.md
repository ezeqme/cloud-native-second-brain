### Excerpt da transcript

For more than 50 years, the European Space Agency has been serving Europe as its space agency and inspiring its citizens. It fulfills its commitment to the peaceful exploration of space through missions ranging from investigations into dark matter to exploring Jupiter's icy moons, gaining a deeper understanding of forests here on Earth and even considering what we might do if there's an asteroid on a collision course with the planet. But ESA has even bolder ambitions. By 2030, ESA wants to significantly increase its number of missions, doubling the number of satellites it flies. And this poses a number of challenges, particularly for the IT infrastructure. Historically, like many legacy applications, the mission control software at ESA was a monolith installed directly on the operating system of physical hardware. To automate and scale up, they want a system that can respond more quickly, reduce downtime, and minimize maintenance windows. They need to be able to make changes to their software and their IT infrastructure even during missions that last for many years.

After reviewing multiple proposals, the European Space Agency selected Canonical for the architecture, design, and delivery of an open source solution that could power its missions into the future. That architecture starts with Canonical's Kubernetes, a highly available platform onto which ESA can deploy its workloads and integrate with its existing IT infrastructure. It's resilient, so that even if disruption happens or a hardware fails, it reduces the impact to running missions. For storage, it relies on Canonical's Charmed Ceph, which provides persistent, scalable storage, and cost savings because no specialized storage hardware is required. Onto this, Canonical has deployed its data and AI products, including PostgreSQL, Kafka, Kubeflow, and Spark. These allow ESA to gain insights from their data and provide infrastructure to the running missions. For ESA, choosing mature open source software was critical. And partnering with Canonical gave it a single service provider to manage the entire on-premise public private cloud and all of these leading open source applications.

At Canonical, we're very proud to be partnering with ESA to explore new frontiers in open source and infrastructure. >> [applause]
